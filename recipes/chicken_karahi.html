Chicken Karahi

Chicken karahi is a popular South Asian dish known for its bold, aromatic flavors and rich tomato-based gravy. Traditionally cooked in a wok-like pan called a karahi, the dish combines chicken with fresh tomatoes, garlic, ginger, green chilies, and a blend of spices that create a slightly tangy and spicy taste.

What makes chicken karahi special is its simplicity and freshness—there’s no heavy marinade or long cooking process. It’s often finished with julienned ginger, green chilies, and fresh cilantro, and served hot with naan or roti, making it a favorite at both home meals and restaurant tables.

Ingredients

  • Chicken (bone-in, cut into pieces)
  • Fresh tomatoes (chopped or crushed)
  • Cooking oil or ghee
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Ginger (minced or julienned)
  • Green chilies
  • Red chili powder
  • Coriander powder
  • Cumin powder
  • Garam masala
  • Salt
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves)

Steps

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a karahi or deep pan and add minced garlic and ginger, sautéing until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and cook on medium heat until they turn white and lightly golden.
  3. Stir in chopped tomatoes, salt, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder.
  4. Cook the mixture until the tomatoes soften and release oil, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add green chilies and a sprinkle of garam masala, then simmer until the chicken is fully cooked and the gravy thickens.
  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro and julienned ginger, and serve hot with naan or roti.
